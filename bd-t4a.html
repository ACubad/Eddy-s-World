The Four Agreements

"The Four Agreements" by Don Miguel Ruiz is a manual for life that could easily fit in the self-help section or with ancient Toltec wisdom texts—if bookstores had such a section. This concise guide offers four principles to practice for personal freedom, happiness, and love. Ruiz distills complex philosophical concepts into straightforward, actionable advice: Be impeccable with your word, don’t take anything personally, don’t make assumptions, and always do your best. It's like the golden rule but upgraded for modern living. The book dives deep into how these agreements can transform your life, freeing you from societal constructs and self-limiting beliefs. It’s a wake-up call to stop living a life scripted by others and start writing your own narrative. If life came with a manual, this book would be the prologue.

Notes from the book

"Be impeccable with your word. Speak with integrity. Say only what you mean." - Words that come out of your mouth don't only affect that who listens to you, you're prone to their effects as well. So be very careful with what you utter.
